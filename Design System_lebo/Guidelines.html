Digital logos

The height of the “v” in the DVT logo can be used to determine the area of isolation around the logo.

Digital logos

The logo must be al least this distance away from the edge of a page and no other design elements may enter this area.

The height of the “v” needs to be the same height as the wording in other logos, regardless of the blue circle. This applies to all other DVT logos.

We aim to bring focus and clarity in our branding without distracting the user. To ensure that the DVT logo integrates seamlessly with the creative use the “V” in DVT to define clear space that is free of type, graphics, and other elements that might cause visual clutter.

The golden ratio

The Golden Ratio is a mathematical ratio used to design harmonious, balanced and aesthetically pleasing composition.

The golden ratio can be achieved by dividing a line into two parts and the longer part (a) divided by the smaller part (b) is equal to the sum of (a) + (b) divided by (a), which both equal 1.618.

The Golden Ratio can be used as a guide on the ideal font combinations you should use for headers and body copy.

Multiply the body copy by 1.618 to get the size of the heading, this also works vice versa.

Eg: 20px body copy multiplied by 1.618 is equal to 32,36 meaning your header text is 32px.

Grid system

A grid system provides a foundation for a harmonious and consistent layout and positioning of elements onscreen

Desktop

In our grid system we apply a centre-aligned 12-column adaptive layout to position the content. The grid has a maximum gutter width of 60px and a column width of 60px on desktop.

This digital style guide uses a 10px gutter and multiples of 30 (30-60-90) to create similar rhythm across all screens. This rhythm is created by the line height between each line of text and the margin between elements.

Spacing guide

This section will highlight how different components interact with each other to create a consistent layout throughout the DVT ecosystem

Headings & paragraphs

Headers and intro headers must be placed 20px apart from each other. Paragraphs must be placed 30px apart from the header/intro header. When you start a new heading underneath a paragraph you can separate them with 90px.

Buttons

Buttons must be placed 30px apart from any content on browsers. If you use buttons either on the right or left side, make sure they are 150px from the side. When buttons are placed next to each other, they must be 60px apart

Checkbox

Each radio button should be 30px apart from one another and the label for each checkbox must be 15px from the checkbox. Radio buttons should be 150px from the side its placed.

Radio Buttons

Each radio button should be 30px apart from one another and the label for each checkbox must be 15px from the checkbox. Radio buttons should be 150px from the side its placed.

Toggle switches

Toggle switches should be 30px apart from one another and the label for each toggle switch must be 10px from the toggle switch. Toggle switches should be 150px from the side its placed. If multiple toggle switches are placed below each other they should be seperated by 30px.

Primary colours

The DVT design system is a living, breathing document that contains all of our visual assets (components, iconography, color palettes, grids, etc).

Colour balance

The overall balance of colours in a design should consist of 20% DVT blue, 5% dark grey and the most prominent colours should be light grey and white. yellow should be used sparingly.
